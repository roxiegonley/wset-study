i

The grape

  • Aromatic white grape, cold-hardy, naturally high acidity.
  • Susceptible to botrytis (noble rot), so it can make quality wine at every sweetness level.
  • Prefers a cool to moderate climate: cool, dry and sunny so sugars and flavours build slowly.
  • Usually fermented in inert vessels (stainless steel) to protect its floral aromas.
  • Off-dry and medium styles: stop fermentation early, or add unfermented juice (Süssreserve).
  • Sweetest styles: from extra-ripe grapes whose high sugar halts fermentation naturally.
  • Can age for years to decades thanks to its high acidity.
ii

Flavour by ripeness

Riesling's flavours shift with how ripe the grapes are picked. Tap each stage.

Just-ripecooler / earlier pick▾
Green fruit (apple, pear), citrus (lemon, lime) and floral (blossom) aromas. Lighter, racier styles.
Extra-ripewarmer / later pick▾
Stone fruit (peach, apricot), tropical fruit (mango, pineapple) and dried fruit. Fuller, often sweeter.
With ageyears in bottle▾
High acidity keeps it fresh while it develops complex tertiary notes of honey and petrol (gasoline) — a positive marker, not a fault.
